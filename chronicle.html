The Handmade Object
Stages a Quiet Return

By Eleanor Ashworth · Filed from the Cotswolds

Across England, a new generation of makers is turning away from mass production in favor of the slow, imperfect, deeply satisfying work of the hand. In workshops hidden among the hills of the Cotswolds, craftsmen are producing furniture that their great-grandchildren might one day inherit. Our correspondent visited three such workshops and found a movement that cares more about joinery than profit margins.
